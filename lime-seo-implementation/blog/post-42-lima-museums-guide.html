Lima Museums Guide — 20 Best Museums with Hours, Prices & Free Days

Lima has over 50 museums spanning pre-Columbian archaeology, colonial art, modern photography, and contemporary installations. Many offer free admission days. Here's your complete museum guide.

2026-04-01 • 9 min read

Top Museums for History & Archaeology

The Larco Museum is Lima's crown jewel — 45,000+ pieces of pre-Columbian art in a stunning 18th-century mansion with beautiful gardens and a cafe. The MALI (Lima Art Museum) has the most comprehensive collection of Peruvian art from pre-Columbian to modern. The Museo de la Nación is massive, always free, and covers Peru's entire cultural history. The Huaca Pucllana in Miraflores is an archaeological site you can visit right in the middle of the city.

Art & Contemporary Museums

The MAC (Contemporary Art Museum) in Barranco showcases Peru's vibrant contemporary art scene. MATE, founded by fashion photographer Mario Testino, features world-class photography exhibitions. The Pedro de Osma Museum in Barranco houses colonial art in a gorgeous mansion. For something different, the LUM (Place of Memory) addresses Peru's internal armed conflict with powerful, moving exhibitions.

Free Museum Days & Budget Tips

The Museo de la Nación is always free. MALI offers free admission on Sundays. Many museums have reduced prices on certain days. Student discounts are widely available (bring your ID). Municipal museums are often free or very cheap. First Sunday of the month, several national museums open for free. A museum day pass doesn't exist, but you can easily visit 2-3 museums in one day.

Practical Tips for Museum Visits

Most museums are open Tuesday-Sunday, 9am-5pm. Mondays are typically closed. Photography policies vary — some allow no-flash photos, others prohibit all photography. Guided tours are available at major museums (worth it at Larco and MALI). Air conditioning is rare — dress comfortably. Allow 1.5-2 hours per museum. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best museum in Lima?

Larco Museum for pre-Columbian art, MALI for comprehensive Peruvian art, MAC for contemporary, and LUM for modern history. Each offers a different experience.

Are Lima museums expensive?

No. Admission ranges from free to S/30 (about $8). Many offer free days and student discounts. You can enjoy several museums for under $20 total.

Can I visit museums with kids?

Absolutely. The Natural History Museum, Larco Museum, and Planetarium are all kid-friendly. Weekend workshops for children are common at major museums.
