Best Cooking Classes in Lima

Several cooking schools cater to tourists with English-speaking instructors. Classes typically start with a market visit, then move to a kitchen where you prepare 3-4 dishes. Top picks include Lima Gourmet Company, Peru Culinary, and Sky Kitchen. Hotel cooking classes are also available at major chains. Group classes run $40-80 per person. Private classes cost more but offer personalized attention.

What You'll Learn to Cook

Most classes cover the classics: ceviche (fresh fish in lime juice), causa (potato terrine), lomo saltado (stir-fried beef), and a pisco sour. Some focus on specific cuisines ‚Äî Amazonian, Andean, or Nikkei (Japanese-Peruvian fusion). Vegetarian and vegan classes are increasingly available. You'll learn techniques you can replicate at home with local ingredients.

Market Tours

Many cooking classes begin with a guided tour of a local market ‚Äî typically Surquillo Market. You'll learn about Peruvian ingredients, taste exotic fruits, and buy the ingredients for your class. This market experience alone is worth the price. You'll discover ingredients like aji amarillo, huacatay, rocoto, and dozens of potato varieties.

Tips for Choosing a Class

Read recent reviews on TripAdvisor. Book in advance during high season (June-September). Ask about dietary accommodations. Afternoon classes are usually less crowded. Some classes include wine or pisco pairings. Budget 3-4 hours for the full experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do cooking classes cost in Lima?

Group classes: $40-80 per person. Private classes: $100-200. Most include market tour, ingredients, recipes, and a full meal with drinks.

Do I need cooking experience?

Not at all. Classes are designed for beginners. Instructors guide you through every step. The focus is on having fun while learning.

Can kids join cooking classes?

Many schools welcome kids (usually 8+). Some offer special family-friendly classes. Contact the school in advance to arrange.
