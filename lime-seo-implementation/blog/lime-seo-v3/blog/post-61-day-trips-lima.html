Day Trips from Lima Peru — 15 Amazing Excursions Within 3 Hours

Lima is the perfect base for unforgettable day trips that take you from ancient ruins to adventure-filled river valleys, wine country, and stunning coastal landscapes — all within a few hours of the city. Whether you're craving history, nature, or adrenaline, these 15 excursions from Lima will show you a side of Peru most tourists never see.

2026-03-01 • 12 min read

Best Day Trips Within 1 Hour of Lima

You don't need to travel far from Lima to find incredible experiences. Some of the best excursions are just 30-60 minutes from the city center, making them perfect for a half-day or morning trip.

Pachacamac Archaeological Complex

Located just 40 minutes south of Lima, Pachacamac is one of the most important archaeological sites on the Peruvian coast. This sprawling complex was a pilgrimage center for over 1,000 years, with temples built by the Lima, Wari, and Inca civilizations. The on-site museum provides excellent context, and the views from the Temple of the Sun are breathtaking.

  • ✓Distance: 31 km south of Lima (40 min by car)
  • ✓Cost: S/15 entrance, S/5 museum
  • ✓Best time: Morning (less crowded, cooler)
  • ✓How to get there: Taxi (S/40-60), organized tour (S/80-150), or colectivo from Lurín

Callao & La Punta — Maritime History

Lima's port district of Callao has reinvented itself as a street art destination and maritime heritage zone. Walk along the Paseo de los Héroes Navales, visit the Real Felipe Fortress (Peru's largest colonial fort), and end at the charming seaside village of La Punta with its seafood restaurants and panoramic views of San Lorenzo Island.

  • ✓Distance: 14 km from Miraflores (30 min)
  • ✓Cost: S/10 fortress entrance
  • ✓Best time: Saturday/Sunday mornings
  • ✓How to get there: Metropolitano + short walk, or taxi (S/20-30)

Caral — The Oldest City in the Americas

A UNESCO World Heritage Site dating back 5,000 years, Caral is older than the Egyptian pyramids. The archaeological site features impressive pyramids, sunken amphitheaters, and evidence of a sophisticated ancient civilization. Located in the Supe Valley, it's about 3 hours from Lima but absolutely worth the early start.

  • ✓Distance: 184 km north of Lima (3 hours)
  • ✓Cost: S/11 entrance
  • ✓Best time: Depart by 7am to avoid afternoon heat
  • ✓How to get there: Organized tour (recommended, $40-80) or rental car via Panamericana Norte

Adventure Day Trips from Lima

For adrenaline seekers, Lima's surrounding geography offers some of the best adventure activities in South America. River rafting, sandboarding, zip-lining, and paragliding are all accessible as day trips from the capital.

Lunahuaná — Rafting & Wine Country

Lunahuaná is Lima's adventure playground, offering Class III-IV rapids on the Cañete River surrounded by stunning Andean foothills. After rafting, explore the local bodegas for pisco and wine tasting — the valley produces some of Peru's finest piscos. The town also offers zip-lining, rappelling, and horseback riding.

  • ✓Distance: 183 km south (2.5-3 hours)
  • ✓Rafting cost: S/60-120 per person
  • ✓Wine tasting: S/20-40 at local bodegas
  • ✓How to get there: Tour operators offer full-day packages (S/150-300), or rent a car

Paracas & Islas Ballestas

The Paracas National Reserve and Ballestas Islands are Peru's answer to the Galápagos. Take a boat tour to see sea lions, Humboldt penguins, and massive colonies of seabirds nesting on dramatic rock formations. Then explore the otherworldly red-sand beaches and desert landscapes of the reserve. It's a long day trip but unforgettable.

  • ✓Distance: 260 km south (3-3.5 hours)
  • ✓Boat tour: S/40-80
  • ✓Reserve entrance: S/11
  • ✓How to get there: Cruz del Sur bus to Paracas (S/40-70), or organized tour from Lima ($80-150)

Beach & Nature Day Trips

Lima sits on one of the world's most dramatic coastlines, and within a couple of hours you can find everything from surf breaks to mangrove wetlands. These nature-focused day trips are perfect for escaping the city without venturing too far.

Punta Hermosa & Punta Rocas — Surfing Paradise

Just 40 minutes south of Lima, these twin beach towns are legendary in the surfing world. Punta Rocas has hosted ISA World Championship events, while Punta Hermosa offers more accessible waves for beginners. Both have a laid-back vibe with beachfront cevicherías and surf shops.

Pantanos de Villa Wildlife Refuge

This surprising urban wetland in Chorrillos is one of Lima's best-kept secrets. Just 30 minutes from Miraflores, the refuge protects 263 hectares of marshland that's home to over 200 bird species. Birdwatchers will love the easy trails and observation platforms.

Cultural & Historical Excursions

Beyond the ancient ruins, the areas surrounding Lima are rich with colonial churches, traditional markets, and living cultural traditions that offer a deeper understanding of Peru's diverse heritage.

Cañete Valley — Afro-Peruvian Heritage

The Cañete Valley, particularly the town of San Luis, is the heartland of Afro-Peruvian culture. Visit during a festival weekend to experience authentic festejo music, traditional dance, and incredible food. The Casa Hacienda San Juan offers guided tours about the region's African heritage.

Canta & Obrajillo — Andean Villages

Located 2.5 hours northeast of Lima in the Chillón Valley, these mountain villages sit at 2,800 meters above sea level. The journey takes you through dramatic landscapes from coastal desert to lush Andean valleys. Once there, enjoy fresh trout, natural swimming pools, and horseback riding.

Practical Tips for Day Trips from Lima

Planning is key for a successful day trip. Start early — most destinations are best visited in the morning before crowds arrive and temperatures rise. Pack sunscreen, water, and snacks regardless of the destination, as Peru's coastal sun is deceptively strong.

For transport, organized tours are the easiest option and often include lunch, but they limit your flexibility. Renting a car gives you freedom but requires confidence with Peruvian driving culture. Public buses (Cruz del Sur, Oltursa) are cheap and comfortable for longer routes to Paracas, Lunahuaná, or Cañete.

Always carry cash in small denominations — many entrance fees, local restaurants, and transport options only accept efectivo. And download offline maps before you go, as cell signal can be spotty outside Lima.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best day trip from Lima Peru?

Paracas and the Ballestas Islands are the most popular and impressive day trip, offering incredible wildlife and dramatic landscapes. For something closer, Pachacamac (40 min) offers world-class archaeology, and Lunahuaná (2.5 hours) is perfect for adventure lovers with rafting and wine tasting.

How do I get to Paracas from Lima for a day trip?

The easiest option is an organized tour ($80-150) that includes transport, boat tour, and reserve visit. You can also take a Cruz del Sur bus (S/40-70, 3.5 hours) to Paracas town and arrange the boat tour locally. Start very early (6am departure) to maximize your time.

Are day trips from Lima safe?

Yes, day trips from Lima are generally safe, especially with organized tours. If traveling independently, stick to main roads, use reputable transport companies, and avoid traveling on remote routes after dark. The popular destinations like Paracas, Lunahuaná, and Pachacamac are well-established tourist routes.

What should I bring on a day trip from Lima?

Essentials include: sunscreen (SPF 50+), water, cash in small denominations, comfortable walking shoes, a hat, and a light jacket (coastal winds can be chilly). For adventure trips, bring a change of clothes. Download offline maps since cell coverage can be limited outside Lima.
