Surfing in Lima Peru — Best Beaches, Schools & Wave Guide

Lima is one of the few major cities in the world where you can surf within city limits. From Makaha beach right below the Miraflores cliffs to world-class breaks further south, Lima's surf scene is vibrant and accessible. Here's everything you need to know.

2026-03-20 • 8 min read

Best Surf Spots in Lima

Makaha and La Herradura are the most accessible — both are within the city and reachable by public transport. Punta Rocas (south of Lima) hosted international competitions and has powerful waves. Punta Hermosa is a surfer town with multiple breaks for all levels. Cerro Azul and San Bartolo are also popular. The water is cold year-round (15-22°C), so a wetsuit is essential.

Surf Schools & Lessons

Several surf schools operate right on the Costa Verde and in Miraflores. Beginner lessons cost $25-50 for 1.5-2 hours including board and wetsuit rental. Most schools have English-speaking instructors. Punta Hermosa also has schools for those wanting a more immersive surf trip. Group lessons are more affordable than private. Most beginners can stand up on their first lesson — Lima waves are forgiving for learning.

Wave Conditions & Best Season

Lima has surfable waves year-round, but the best conditions are from April to October (winter swells from the south). Summer (Dec-Mar) has smaller, warmer waves — good for beginners. The water is always cold thanks to the Humboldt Current. Morning sessions are best — winds pick up in the afternoon. Swell direction is primarily from the southwest. Check surf forecast apps for daily conditions.

Gear Rental & Surf Culture

Board and wetsuit rental is available at all major surf spots — about S/20-40 per hour. Several surf shops in Miraflores sell and rent quality gear. Lima's surf culture is strong — local competitions, surf film screenings, and beach community events happen regularly. The Peruvian surfing community is welcoming to newcomers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can beginners surf in Lima?

Absolutely. Several beaches like Makaha have gentle waves perfect for learning. Multiple surf schools offer beginner lessons with all gear included.

Do I need a wetsuit to surf in Lima?

Yes, always. The water temperature ranges from 15-22°C year-round thanks to the Humboldt Current. A 3/2mm wetsuit is standard.

How much do surf lessons cost in Lima?

Expect $25-50 for a 1.5-2 hour lesson including board and wetsuit. Group lessons are cheaper. Multi-day packages offer better rates.
